Introduction
Google Drawings is a graphical utility centered around customizable pre-defined shapes. Dozens of these are available, and can be layered atop images or combined to form diagrams, among other applications. As with all other Google Drive utilities, Google Drawings is entirely web-based, requiring only a web browser to run, and saves documents automatically in the cloud.
Interface
Google Drawings consists primarily of a 2 menu bars and a canvas. All images and shapes are created and manipulated within the canvas. Various shapes and lines can be inserted by navigating through the shape and line menus. The text box icon allows for quick text box creation.
Creating a New Google Drawing
Google Drawings must be created from a specific page before being modified.
1.
Go to “https://drive.google.com” and log in with your Google account.
2.
Click the blue “NEW” button in the upper left-hand corner, then click “Google Sheets”.
3.
Click the text field with “Untitled document” and type in a name. Press enter or click anywhere outside the box to save.
Accessing Google Drawings
Once created, Google Drawings can be accessed from the main Google Drive page.
1.
Go to “https://drive.google.com” and log in with your Google account.
2.
Scroll down until you see the Google Drawing you want to access.
3.
Double click on the Google Drawing's icon.
Sharing Google Drawings
If you want others to be able to view, edit, or comment on a Google Drawing you are working on, you will need to share it with them. The procedure for sharing a Sheet is simple and requires only their Gmail address or other email address associated with a Google account.
1.
Open the Google Drawing you wish to share.
2.
Click the blue “Share” button in the upper right-hand corner. The following should appear:
3.
Type the email address of the person you want to share the Drawing with into the text box. The email address must be a Gmail one or be associated with a Google account.
4.
Click on the pencil icon to the right of the text box, then click the box corresponding to what you want to allow the person to do to the Drawing (edit, comment, or view).
5.
Click the “Done” box.
Downloading Google Drawings
Google Drawings can be saved as PDF, PNG, JPG, and SVG files.
1.
Click the "File" tab in the top menu bar.
2.
Hover your cursor over "Download as", then select the format you wish to export the Drawing as. Generally, the PNG format is a safe option as it saves images with minimal quality loss.
Printing Google Drawings
Google Drawings can easily be printed straight from the browser.
1.
Press the "CTRL" key and "P" simultaneously. Your web browser's standard print page will appear.
2.
Adjust settings as necessary and print the Google Drawing.
Viewing the Version History
Google Drawings automatically keeps a record of changes made to every document. You can view the dates of these changes along with who made them and a snapshot of the spreadsheet at that time by accessing its Version History.
1.
Click "All changes saved in Drive" located immediately to the right of the File/Edit/View... menu bar. The Version History interface will appear.
2.
Click on any date to view the Google Drawing as it existed then.
3.
Click the back arrow at the top left of the page to return to the standard Google Drawings interface.
Inserting Images into Google Drawings
Google Drawings is often used to annotate images and allows for easy image insertion.
From the Internet
1.
Click the image icon on the second menu bar.
2.
Hover your cursor over "Image", then select "By URL". A window will appear.
3.
Copy and paste the image URL into the text box, then press "Insert".
From Your Computer
1.
Click the image icon on the second menu bar.
2.
Hover your cursor over "Image", then select "Upload from computer". A window will appear.
3.
Locate the image on your computer, select it, and press "Open". Note that the exact interface may vary by operating system.
Inserting Text
Google Drawings' text box tools allows for easy and flexible text insertion.
1.
Click the text box icon on the menu bar.
2.
Within the canvas, click where you want the text box to begin, then, with the mouse button held down, drag in the direction you want the text box to extend.
3.
Type as desired into the text box.
Shape Basics
Google Drawings is centered around pre-defined shapes you can access from the shape and line menus. The procedure for adding any of these to the canvas is essentially the same.
1.
Click either the shape or the line menu icon within the second menu bar.
2.
Hover over any shape category, then click on a shape.
3.
Within the canvas, click where you want the shape to begin, then, with the mouse button held down, drag in the direction you want the shape to extend.
