Creating a Header
By creating a header, you can determine what text goes at the top of each page. This is useful for labeling pages with information such as name, date, and page number.
1.
Click the “Insert” tab in the top menu bar.
2.
Click “Header”. A line will appear about an inch from the top of the page and it will be possible to enter text from the top of the page to where text usually begins as dictated by margins.
3.
Type whatever you wish to have in the header, adjusting alignment and other formatting options as desired.
Creating a Footer
By creating a footer, you can determine what text goes at the bottom of each page. The footer is usually used to store information such as name and page number.
1.
Click the “Insert” tab in the top menu bar.
2.
Click “Footer”. A line will appear about an inch from the bottom of the page and it will be possible to enter text from the bottom of the page to where text usually ends as dictated by margins.
3.
Type whatever you wish to have in the footer, adjusting alignment and other formatting options as desired.
Creating a Hanging Indent
To indent all lines beyond the first in a block of text, create a hanging indent. Hanging indents are commonly used to format bibliographies.
1.
Highlight all text you want indented.
2.
Click the blue triangle with a blue rectangle above it on the numbered menu bar just above the white document field, and, with the mouse button held down, move it as far right as you want lines to be indented.
3.
Click the blue rectangle above the previously moved blue triangle, and, with the mouse button held down, move it left to where it was before Step 2.
Inserting an Image
Images from both the internet and your computer can easily be added to Google Docs documents.
From the Internet
1.
Click the “Insert” tab in the top menu bar.
2.
Hover your cursor over "Image", then select "By URL". A window will appear.
3.
Copy and paste the image URL into the text box, then press "Insert".
From Your Computer
1.
Click the “Insert” tab in the top menu bar.
2.
Hover your cursor over "Image", then select "Upload from computer". A window will appear.
3.
Locate the image on your computer, select it, and press "Open". Note that the exact interface may vary by operating system.
Adjusting Margins
Page margins can be modified if default values are not suitable.
1.
Click the “File” tab in the top menu bar.
2.
Click “Page setup…”.
3.
Double click within the text boxes corresponding to the margins you want to modify, then type in different values. The greater the number, the more white space there will be from the specified direction.
4.
Press "OK" to save changes.
