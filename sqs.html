Introduction
Google Sheets is Google’s version of Microsoft Excel. As with all other Google Drive utilities, it is entirely web-based, requiring only a web browser to run, and saves documents automatically in the cloud. The software is ideal for data entry and manipulation as well as for data visualization with dozens of customizable graphs available. .
Interface
Google Sheets consists primarily of a spreadsheet, 2 menu bars, and an equation line. The most important component is the spreadsheet. All data is stored within the cells of the spreadsheet. Note the vertical numeric cell labeling and horizontal alphabetic cell labeling. Cells are referenced as a combination of these two labels. For instance, the first cell is ‘A1’, the one immediately to the right of it ‘B1’, and the one immediately below ‘A1’ ‘A2’. Graphs can be added to the spreadsheet through the ‘Insert’ tab.
Creating a New Google Sheet
Google Sheets must be created from a specific page before being modified.
1.
Go to “https://drive.google.com” and log in with your Google account.
2.
Click the blue “NEW” button in the upper left-hand corner, then click “Google Sheets”.
3.
Click the text field with “Untitled document” and type in a name. Press enter or click anywhere outside the box to save.
Accessing Google Sheets
Once created, Google Sheets can be accessed from the main Google Drive page.
1.
Go to “https://drive.google.com” and log in with your Google account.
2.
Scroll down until you see the Google Sheet you want to access.
3.
Double click on the Google Sheet's icon.
Sharing Google Sheets
If you want others to be able to view, edit, or comment on a Google Sheet you are working on, you will need to share it with them. The procedure for sharing a Sheet is simple and requires only their Gmail address or other email address associated with a Google account.
1.
Open the Google Sheet you wish to share.
2.
Click the blue “Share” button in the upper right-hand corner. The following should appear:
3.
Type the email address of the person you want to share the Sheet with into the text box. The email address must be a Gmail one or be associated with a Google account.
4.
Click on the pencil icon to the right of the text box, then click the box corresponding to what you want to allow the person to do to the Sheet (edit, comment, or view).
5.
Click the “Done” box.
Downloading Google Sheets
Google Sheets can be saved as PDF and .xlsx files, among other formats.
1.
Click the "File" tab in the top menu bar.
2.
Hover your cursor over "Download as", then select the format you wish to export the document as. Generally, the PDF format is a safe option if the Google Sheet is complete and you want to best preserve the appearance, while .xlsx and .csv are good choices if you want to work with data stored in the Google Sheet.
Printing Google Sheets
Google Sheets can easily be printed straight from the browser.
1.
Press the "CTRL" key and "P" simultaneously. Your web browser's standard print page will appear.
2.
Adjust settings as necessary and print the Google Sheet.
Viewing the Version History
Google Sheets automatically keeps a record of changes made to every document. You can view the dates of these changes along with who made them and a snapshot of the spreadsheet at that time by accessing its Version History.
1.
Click "All changes saved in Drive" located immediately to the right of the File/Edit/View... menu bar. The Version History interface will appear.
2.
Click on any date to view the Google Sheet as it existed then.
3.
Click the back arrow at the top left of the page to return to the standard Google Sheets interface.
Inputting Data
Before using Google Sheets for anything else, you will need to input data.
1.
Double-click on the cell you want to input the data into. The cell should become highlighted and a blinking “|” should be present.
2.
Type in the data you wish to enter.
3.
Save by pressing enter or clicking outside the cell.
Modifying a Cell’s Data
Data present in cells can be easily be modified.
1.
Double-click on the cell with data you want to modify. The cell should become highlighted and a blinking “|” should be present.
2.
Use your arrow keys and/or cursor to move the text cursor, delete data with the backspace key, and enter data by typing it.
3.
Save by pressing enter or clicking outside the cell.
Deleting a Cell’s Data
If you want to remove all the data in a cell, there is a more efficient way to do so.
1.
Click on the cell whose data you wish to remove. The cell should become highlighted.
2.
Press the backspace key.
