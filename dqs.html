Introduction
Google Docs is a free cloud-based word processing utility roughly equivalent to Microsoft Word. Documents are created from Google Drive and can be accessed wherever you have internet access. You can easily share documents with anyone with a Google account and collaborate on documents with others.
Interface
Google Docs has a simple, fairly standard interface. Most important settings can be adjusted from the main page without entering any of the File/Edit/View/Insert/Format/ Tools/Table/Add-ons/Help menus. These include the document title, font type, font size, text style (bold, italic, underlined), the text and highlight color, and text alignment.
Creating a New Document
Google Docs documents must be created from a specific page before being modified.
1.
Go to “https://drive.google.com” and log in with your Google account.
2.
Click the blue “NEW” button in the upper left-hand corner, then click “Google Docs”.
3.
Click the text field with “Untitled document” and type in a document name. Press enter or click anywhere outside the box to save.
Accessing Documents
Once created, Google Docs documents can be accessed from the main Google Drive page.
1.
Go to “https://drive.google.com” and log in with your Google account.
2.
Scroll down until you see the document you want to access.
3.
Double click on the document’s icon.
Sharing Documents
If you want others to be able to view, edit, or comment on a document you are working on, you will need to share it with them. The procedure for sharing a document is simple and requires only their Gmail address or other email address associated with a Google account.
1.
Open the document you wish to share.
2.
Click the blue “Share” button in the upper right-hand corner. The following should appear:
3.
Type the email address of the person you want to share the document with into the text box. The email address must be a Gmail one or be associated with a Google account.
4.
Click on the pencil icon to the right of the text box, then click the box corresponding to what you want to allow the person to do to the document (edit, comment, or view).
5.
Click the “Done” box.
Downloading Documents
Google Docs documents can be saved as PDF and .docx files, among other formats.
1.
Click the "File" tab in the top menu bar.
2.
Hover your cursor over "Download as", then select the format you wish to export the document as. Generally, the PDF format is a safe option if the document is complete, while .docx is ideal for incomplete documents.
Printing Documents
Documents can easily be printed straight from Google Docs.
1.
Press the "CTRL" key and "P" simultaneously. Your web browser's standard print page will appear.
2.
Adjust settings as necessary and print the document.

Viewing the Version History
Google Docs automatically keeps a record of changes made to every document. You can view the dates of these changes along with who made them and a snapshot of the document at that time by accessing its Version History.
1.
Click "All changes saved in Drive" located immediately to the right of the File/Edit/View... menu bar. The Version History interface will appear.
2.
Click on any date to view the document as it existed then.
3.
Click the back arrow at the top left of the page to return to the document.
